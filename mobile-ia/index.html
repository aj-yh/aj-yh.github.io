Refreshing the Mobile App information architecture

Role

  • Drove cross-team alignment and stakeholder buy-in to move project forward
  • Collaborated with product and engineering leaders to plan and execute a seamless rollout
  • Provided ongoing design feedback and shaped the overall direction

Credit goes to Faye Xu for her work on this project. The final IA and UI/UX are the product of her hard work! Special thanks to Jen Cotton for helping get this initiative prioritized and keeping us accountable along the way.

Context

Through years of individual feature launches without a holistic plan, the GoodRx Mobile App had become a bit of a Frankenstein’s monster. The landing pages for each navigation tab did not serve clear purposes. For example, Home had become a hodgepodge of medication management, saved items, promotions, and more. With new product launches ever approaching, it became clear that we needed a more rational information architecture to be able to appropriately scale and surface things in the appropriate place.

Auditing

In order to understand the full scope of the information architecture, extensive auditing and diagramming was necessary to map out the relationship of different pages, features, and objects to each other. Through this process, we understood where pockets of complexity lay, and could start creating more rational frameworks for organizing the necessary pieces.

Diverging

With a better understanding of the full scope, we put together three divergent directions we could user-test and present to senior stakeholders. This exercise helped us avoid committing to a particular direction too early in the process and built confidence that our final direction was the right one.

Converging

Through extensive cross-team conversation, synthesizing research results, and diving deep into the details, we aligned on a solution that combined the best parts of the three original directions. Then, in collaboration with designers from different teams, we created engineering-ready designs for all the key screens, states, and flows. The initial version of this IA refresh launched in late 2025, and further slices are continuing to release through 2026.

Impact

The immediate focus for this project was enabling future scalability, in alignment with the company’s long-term strategy around new feature launches and business lines. The IA that launched was successful in creating that scalable framework, and also established a governance system for ensuring continued coherence over time.

While the metrics we tracked were positioned as guardrails, we actually drove meaningful improvement to key metrics as well. These tangible improvements helped prove out the theory that UX- and scalability-focused changes can also deliver material business benefits, in addition to internal enablement. Some of the metric improvements included:

iOS

  • Total authentication increased by ~11%, account creation by ~7%, and logged-in rate by ~5%
  • Search completion increased by 4%
  • Price page views (a key business metric) increased by ~11%

Android

  • Total authentication increased by ~12%, account creation by ~8%, and logged-in rate by ~4%
  • Search completion increased by 12%
  • Price page views (a key business metric) increased by ~17%
